Not just a few — **consistent, recent, high-quality reviews**.

For small businesses in Monmouth County, reviews directly impact:

- your Google rankings (especially Maps)
- whether people trust you
- whether someone calls you or your competitor

The difference between a business with 12 reviews and one with 85+ is massive.

This guide breaks down exactly how to get more Google reviews — without being pushy or awkward.

## 1. Ask at the Right Time (This Is Everything)

Most businesses don’t get reviews because they **don’t ask** — or they ask at the wrong time.

Best time to ask:

- right after a successful job
- when the customer is clearly happy
- immediately after delivering value

Not:

- weeks later
- randomly
- without context

Timing alone can double your review rate.

---

## 2. Make It Ridiculously Easy

If it takes effort, people won’t do it.

You should:

- send a direct Google review link
- avoid asking people to “find you on Google”
- remove all friction

Pro tip:
Create a short link or QR code for reviews.

---

## 3. Use a Simple Script (Don’t Overthink It)

You don’t need anything complicated.

Example:

“Hey, if you were happy with everything, would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? It really helps our business.”

That’s it.

No pressure. No long explanation.

---

## 4. Follow Up Once (Most Businesses Don’t)

People forget — not ignore.

A simple follow-up like:

“Just wanted to resend this in case you missed it — we’d really appreciate a quick review if you have a minute.”

This alone can significantly increase responses.

---

## 5. Respond to Every Review

This is huge — and most businesses ignore it.

When you respond:

- it shows professionalism
- it builds trust
- it improves local SEO signals

Include:

- the service
- the location (naturally)

Example:
“Thanks for trusting us with your website redesign in Monmouth County — we appreciate it!”

---

## 6. Get Keywords in Reviews (Naturally)

You should never script reviews — but you can guide them.

If a customer mentions:

- the service
- the location

That helps your rankings.

Instead of:
“Great service”

You want:
“Great website design company in Monmouth County”

---

## 7. Be Consistent (Not Random)

Getting 20 reviews in one month and then none for a year isn’t ideal.

Google prefers:

- steady, ongoing activity

Aim for:

- 2–5 reviews per month consistently

---

## 8. Avoid Fake Reviews (Seriously)

Buying reviews or incentivizing them can:

- get your profile suspended
- destroy trust

It’s not worth it.

Authentic reviews always win long-term.

---

## 9. Use Reviews Across Your Website

Don’t let reviews live only on Google.

Add them to:

- your homepage
- service pages
- contact page

This improves conversions significantly.
